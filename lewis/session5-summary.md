# Session 5 Summary — Hammarby

## Where We Left Off

Lewis is at the estate, seven days from a meeting northeast of the spring. Saoirse has made contact. The queen's survey is two baronies away. He has until then to decide which is more urgent — but he has already decided.

---

## What Happened This Session

A downtime session. The estate reasserted its gravity.

Oskar led the tilling of the north field without ceremony. On the second day he pocketed something turned up by the plough — an old bone or marker — without mentioning it to Lewis. Lewis didn't ask.

A boundary dispute between the Knutsson family and old Bernt came to a head. Lewis heard both sides the same afternoon and ruled for Bernt: longer tenure, better records, and the Knutsson son had driven stakes in the dark. The Knutssons accepted it the way people accept rulings they intend to revisit. The son said nothing.

Maret began leaving small offerings at the corner post of the north field each morning — tied grass, nothing dramatic. Lewis noticed and said nothing.

Lewis cleaned and oiled his sword alone by the fire. The charm from the north field went on a nail by his room door, carrying Aldric's name now.

Lewis told Theodor something about the spring — what surfaced there, what he had admitted to himself about the Harrying. Theodor listened and said: *"You are still here. That is something. Most men who did what you did are still doing it."* Lewis had no answer.

News arrived that the queen's census agents are active two baronies south. Theodor's soil drainage report is ready; it will hold anyone who doesn't already know to look for something.

The morning after, Edith delivered a preserved crow with bark markings — Saoirse's contact. Northeast, past the spring, seven days' time. Lewis goes.
