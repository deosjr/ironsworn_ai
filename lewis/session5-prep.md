# Session 5 Preparation — Hammarby

## Where We Left Off
Lewis ended session 4 at the estate. The north field is sealed. Bram is dead. Saoirse has returned to the forest. A new vow has been sworn. Spirit is at 2 — Lewis needs recovery before anything demanding.

Momentum: 6. Health: 4. Spirit: 2. Supply: 3. Experience: 1.

---

## The Shape of This Session

A downtime session. No thin places, no combat, no druids. The estate reasserts its own gravity. Lewis recovers — not through rest exactly, but through the ordinary work of being a lord.

Saoirse's contact arrives at the end, coinciding with news of the queen's survey. The session closes with two threats arriving simultaneously and Lewis having to decide which is more urgent.

---

## Beat 1 — The Field

Oskar leads the tilling. Lewis watches from a distance the first morning, then stops watching. The peasants don't celebrate. They just work.

It should feel like a quiet exhale — undramatic, almost anticlimactic. This is what the vow was for. It doesn't feel like enough, which is exactly right.

Something turns up in the first tilling — a bone, an old marker, something Oskar pockets without mentioning to Lewis. Small. Unresolved.

---

## Beat 2 — The Boundary Dispute

Something ordinary goes wrong that has nothing to do with thin places. A boundary dispute that turns ugly, or a judgment Lewis doesn't feel qualified to make. The estate has its own logic and its own grievances that predate Lewis and will outlast him.

Lewis has to act as a lord. He may not get it right. Consequences are slow-burning — Njáls saga rhythm.

---

## Beat 3 — Maret (background, ongoing)

Maret starts leaving a small offering at the field's edge each morning. Nothing dramatic. No announcement. Lewis notices and says nothing. Neither does she.

This is not a scene. It is a detail that surfaces across multiple scenes — something Lewis keeps catching at the edge of his attention. The old ways moving sideways into his household without anyone deciding it.

---

## Beat 4 — The Sword

Lewis cleans and oils the sword himself. A small private ritual. The sword has been in a lake and in a tomb and needs attention. He does it alone, probably in the evening.

---

## Beat 5 — The Charm

The woven charm from the north field — carried since session 1 — goes into Lewis's room. He keeps it on a shelf or a nail. It was placed as a warning; he reads it as a ward. It is neither exactly, but it carries Aldric's name now and that gives it weight enough.

---

## Beat 6 — Theodor

Lewis and Theodor have a real conversation. Not logistics. Lewis tells him something about the spring — what surfaced there, what he admitted to himself about the Harrying.

Theodor listens without filling the silence. He doesn't offer absolution or judgment. He says something careful and true that Lewis doesn't have an answer to.

This scene lands harder after the boundary dispute — after Lewis has had to make a judgment he wasn't ready for. Schedule it accordingly.

---

## Beat 7 — The Survey

News arrives that the queen's survey is closer than expected. A traveller mentions it in passing, or Theodor receives a letter. Not arrival — just evidence of approach. The paperwork suddenly feels less theoretical.

Theodor's carefully boring soil report will need to hold up under actual scrutiny. He may ask Lewis something uncomfortable about what exactly is in that report.

---

## Closing Beat — Saoirse

Saoirse makes contact just as the survey news arrives. Not in person — a message, a sign, something in the old tradition that Lewis has learned enough to read or ask Edith to read.

The session ends with two things arriving at once. Lewis has to decide which is more urgent.
