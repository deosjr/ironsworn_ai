# WORLD DOCUMENT — Ironsworn Campaign

## Concept
A dark historical fantasy blending Roman Britain (~100AD) with post-Conquest England (~1070AD). Two cultural strata exist:
- **The Conquerors** (Roman/Norman blend): civic polytheism, state priests, pragmatic and militaristic, recently victorious
- **The Natives** (Celtic/Anglo-Saxon blend): old gods, druids, scattered and resentful, holders of deep mythic knowledge

The world is based loosely on the historical Harrying of the North, reframed as a fantasy setting. Geography resembles central Sweden (Uppsala region) rather than northern England — low rolling landscape, mixed forest, lakes, open fields.

---

## The Mythic Crisis: The Fifth Season

The native culture practiced **sacred kingship**: each year the king ritually wed a chosen maiden. The maiden was sacrificed in autumn to ensure the seasonal cycle continued. The king himself was sacrificed only when disaster struck (crop failure etc). This ritual kept the boundary between the living world and the underworld sealed.

The new Conqueror king refused to participate — the marriage, the sacrifice, and especially his own potential death are all unacceptable to him. He has declared total victory after the Harrying and considers the matter closed.

As a result, the seasonal cycle is breaking down. A **Fifth Season** — of endless mist — is growing longer each year. In myth this has happened before, but no living person has seen it or knows how it ended. The fifth season causes the boundary between the living world and the underworld to dissolve. Strange, unknowable things cross over — more Lovecraftian than corporeal. Ghosts, presences, things that should not be here. Not zombies or tangible undead, at least not yet.

The druids still perform the maiden ritual, knowing it is insufficient without the full sacred kingship cycle. They are messengers and interpreters of the gods, not power brokers — they have no hidden agenda beyond fulfilling their religious duty.

### What Is Known (as of Session 3)
The fifth season's cause has been confirmed from two independent sources — Edith in session 1, and Conlaoch in session 2. The king's refusal of the sacred kingship ritual is directly responsible. The boundary is thinning everywhere, not just at Hammarby; the north field is one thin place among others, and there will be more.

Thin places can be sealed individually through a localised ritual — something given willingly to what lies beneath, performed by someone who knows the old ways. This does not address the fifth season itself, only its local symptoms.

The north field at Hammarby is a **thin place** — the boundary has worn through there. It has been wrong for two seasons, made worse by the Harrying. Aldric died there when something from beneath looked at him directly. His heart stopped.

A sealing ritual is now in progress. Saoirse has sent Lewis into a tomb northeast of Hammarby to retrieve an ancient sword — something taken inside when the boundary first began to fail. She will use it to seal the north field. Lewis is currently inside the tomb.

## Factions

### The Conqueror King
Declared victory. Willfully blind to the mythic crisis. Considers the old religion barbaric. Will resist any information contradicting his narrative of total control.

### The Queen
The most politically dangerous figure. Does not share her husband's complacency. Has caught wind of occult whispers and believes the druids represent a real threat — framing it as political rather than cosmic. Under cover of a national survey (equivalent of the Domesday Book), she is sending agents across the land to gather intelligence and wage a shadow war against the druids. Her inquisition is real and growing. She is a believer, but probably does not understand the true nature of what she is fighting.

### The Druids
Keepers of the old ritual. They choose the maiden each year, perform the ceremonies, and watch the fifth season worsen. They are not villains — they are functionaries of something larger than themselves, increasingly desperate. They are deeply resentful of the conquest and the Harrying. They would collaborate with the Conquerors to avert the fifth season if trust were possible, but it is not — yet.

Saoirse is the first druid Lewis has met directly. The exchange so far is transactional. Whether it becomes something more depends on what Lewis brings out of the tomb, and how.

They operate in the forest north and west of Hammarby.

### The Displaced Natives
An informal network of people dispossessed by the Harrying — those who lost land, family, and livelihood and did not flee north. They survive in the margins: forests, abandoned steadings, the edges of Conqueror estates. They watch. They share information.

Two distinct factions have emerged within this network:

- **Conlaoch's faction**: pragmatic. The fifth season is the real enemy. Willing to find a Conqueror who will listen and work with them.
- **Bram's faction**: wants blood for blood. The Harrying demands vengeance, not negotiation. They regard Conlaoch as a fool or a traitor. They have been watching Hammarby with intent, not just information-gathering.

Bram — the prisoner Lewis took after the Langsjön ambush — belonged to the second faction. He is currently in Rolf's custody at the estate, awaiting execution on Lewis's return.

### The Displaced Native Army
North of the border. Resentful, eager to reconquer their homeland, but currently powerless to act. A pressure that could become kinetic.

### The Rejected Maiden
Each year the druids still elect a maiden and perform what rituals they can, knowing it is insufficient without the king's participation. The maiden is still sacrificed in autumn. However, without the full sacred kingship cycle being observed, the local population increasingly questions the purpose — rumours circulate that the druids are simply abducting young women. At any time there is at most one living maiden, in the druids' keeping until autumn.

## Religion

### Conqueror Religion
Civic polytheism modeled on Roman practice. Priests are ritual administrators rather than spiritual guides — managing sacrifice, augury, temple ceremonies. The state cult practices *interpretatio* — absorbing native gods by renaming them as equivalents of their own pantheon. Tolerates old gods as long as new names are used and official priests are acknowledged. This makes druidic resistance simultaneously religious and political.

### Native Religion
Old gods, seasonal cycles, sacred kingship. The druids are interpreters and ritual specialists, not rulers. The religion is deeply tied to the land and its cycles.

The sacred spring northeast of Hammarby is one of the religion's liminal places — it requires honest self-examination before passage. It cannot be deceived. Lewis passed through it in session 3.

## The Estate

### Situation
A ruined estate on the northern border, granted to Lewis of Mervik after the Harrying. Once prosperous, now largely destroyed. Lewis is expected to rebuild it from nothing.

The fifth season is already affecting it. The north field is a thin place. The sealing ritual is underway — Lewis is currently in the tomb retrieving what Saoirse needs. If the ritual succeeds, the field can be tilled before harvest.

### Geography
Modeled on the landscape around Linnaeus' Hammarby, southeast of Uppsala: low rolling terrain, mixed forest, open fields between tree lines, lakes. Quiet and melancholy.

**Langsjön** — a long narrow lake at one edge of the estate. The estate sits at one end; the far end disappears into forest and mist. Water is liminal in native myth. The druids have a relationship with this lake — their contact point with Lewis was a tall grey standing stone where the forest meets the eastern bank at the lake's northern end.

**The North Field** — a thin place. The boundary between the living world and the underworld is worn through here. The peasants have not tilled it for two seasons. Aldric died at its edge.

**The Forest** — presses in from the north and west, thickening toward the border. The druids operate within it. Key locations discovered in session 3:

- *The Clearing* — circular, too regular to be natural, mist staying at its treeline. The neutral ground where Lewis met Saoirse and Conlaoch.
- *The Sacred Spring* — an hour north of the clearing. A dark pool in a hollow. It requires honest self-examination; a man who lies to himself cannot pass through it cleanly. Lewis passed, at cost.
- *The Charm Boundary* — a clearing hung with woven charms of different ages, tended over years. Marks the threshold before the tomb.
- *The Tomb* — a low mound older than the conquest. A single passage leading to a chamber. Conlaoch sealed the entrance before Lewis arrived — recently, using warning charms of the same type as the north field. A displaced standing stone at the entrance bears runes and a carved map of the interior. An ancient rusted sword rests on a stone shelf in the chamber.

### Estate Inventory (After Session 3)
- Hall: stone foundation intact, partial roof now in place
- Village of 31 able-bodied native peasants
- Three soldiers: Rolf (oldest, trusted), Edde (younger, eager), Maret (quiet, sharp)
- Two clercs: Theodor (older administrator), and a younger scribe not yet developed
- Supplies reduced (Supply: 3)
- One horse (Bally, at the estate)
- One prisoner: Bram, bound at the estate in Rolf's custody. Awaiting execution on Lewis's return.

### Feudal Obligations
Lewis owes feudal levy to a nearby baron who is himself managing ruined estates and not yet collecting taxes. The levy for an estate this size is effectively just Lewis himself — expected to arrive with horse and armour. He has the horse. The armour is a future problem.

## Named NPCs

### Edith (Hammarby Village)
Oldest woman in the village. Present since before the Harrying. The other peasants defer to her without making a show of it. Sharp, composed, not easily frightened. Knows more than she says. Served as Saoirse's messenger in session 3, delivering the formal invitation to Lewis. Knew about Oskar and the food supply all along. Told Lewis to execute Bram plainly — *don't dress it up as justice, these people know the difference.*

### Theodor (Hammarby, Older Clerc)
Lewis's administrator. Competent, careful, quietly opinionated. Knows everything Lewis knows. His counsel: handle the druid quietly, no record, one thing at a time. Can write official reports in a way that deflects scrutiny. Effectively Lewis's most trusted confidant by default.

### Oskar (Hammarby Village)
An older farmer with a strip in the fallow north field. Lost a son in the hard winter after the Harrying. Has been leaving food at the treeline for Conlaoch since autumn — pragmatism, not ideology. Lewis confronted him directly in session 3 and let him continue openly. Not an enemy.

### Conlaoch (the Forest)
A native survivor of the Harrying who lost his wife, children, and farm. Has been living in the forest near the estate, sustained by Oskar's food. He placed the warning charm at the north field and has been watching the estate for months.

Brought Saoirse to the clearing as arranged. Accompanied Lewis to the sacred spring and waited outside. Also sealed the tomb entrance before Lewis arrived — using the same warning-charm tradition — without telling Lewis he intended to do so. His reasons are unclear.

He omitted the existence of Bram's faction — people who actively want Lewis dead. That omission is harder to excuse than merely not mentioning the network. Lewis has not yet raised it with him.

### Saoirse (the Forest, Druid)
A druid who knows a rite to seal thin places and has done so before. Older than Lewis expected. Met Lewis in the clearing in session 3. The exchange: Lewis retrieves an object from the tomb; she seals the north field. Required him to pass through the sacred spring first. Told him the tomb would know if he lied to himself.

Transactional so far. What she makes of Lewis — the first Conqueror to sit with her on her ground — is unclear.

### Bram (Hammarby, prisoner)
A displaced native who ambushed Lewis on the Langsjön path. Part of the faction that believes Conlaoch is wasting his time negotiating with conquerors. Said nothing during two days of questioning. Lewis has decided to execute him plainly on his return from the tomb.

## Key Relationships

### The Saved General
Lewis distinguished himself saving this general in combat, which earned him Hammarby. The general now manages multiple estates in the south and has largely forgotten Lewis exists. The connection is real but effectively inert.

### The Local Baron
Lewis's immediate feudal superior. Managing his own reconstruction. Not yet demanding taxes or levy. Relationship not yet developed.

## Tragic Structure

The core tragedy: averting the fifth season requires collaboration between Conquerors and natives. The druids have the knowledge; the king has the power to restore the ritual. But the conquest and Harrying have made trust nearly impossible. Lewis, positioned between both worlds as a lowborn soldier elevated into Conqueror nobility, may be the only person capable of eventually brokering this — if he survives long enough to understand what is actually needed.

Session 1: Lewis sat on a small stool in Edith's home and listened instead of threatened.
Session 2: Lewis held his temper at the standing stone and kept the conversation alive. A druid is coming.
Session 3: Lewis sat with what he did in the Harrying and did not look away. He is in the tomb now, alone, reaching for something ancient in the dark.
