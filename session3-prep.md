# Session 3 Preparation — Hammarby

## Where We Left Off
Lewis ended session 2 at the estate, having carried an unconscious prisoner back from Langsjön. He told Theodor everything. The prisoner is bound outside the hall. Morning has not yet come.

Momentum: 10. Health: 5. Spirit: 5. Supply: 5.

---

## The Soldiers

Three named soldiers, all that remain after Aldric:

- **Rolf** — oldest, been with Lewis longest. Knew Aldric. Lewis confided in him minimally when handing over the prisoner. Knows more than the others but isn't pushing.
- **Edde** — younger, eager. Has been on watch all night with a prisoner he knows nothing about. Tired and curious by morning.
- **Maret** — the quiet one. Possibly the most dangerous thinker of the three.

---

## The Prisoner

A displaced native turned bandit. Knew Lewis by name and face before a word was exchanged — had been watching the estate. Said *we are everywhere* before being knocked unconscious.

Lewis's intention: keep him alive as a bargaining chip, at least until the druid arrives. The man attacked him and should rightfully be put to the sword — patience here is practical, not merciful.

He has no reason to cooperate. Lewis has Shadow 1 and no leverage yet. A Compel attempt will be difficult.

---

## The Two Factions

The displaced natives are not unified. Two distinct positions:

- **Con's faction**: pragmatic, wants to find a Conqueror willing to restore the balance. The fifth season is the real enemy.
- **The prisoner's faction**: wants blood for blood. The Harrying demands vengeance, not negotiation.

Con omitting the existence of this second faction — and that part of it actively wants Lewis dead — is an omission harder to excuse than merely not mentioning the network.

---

## The Village Contact

Someone in the village has been leaving food at the treeline for Con through winter. Lewis now knows about it openly, per his arrangement with Con.

The contact is most likely a pragmatist whose self-interest aligns with Con's: a farmer with strips in the north field who wants it back in production. Not ideology — practical alignment.

Edith knew. Whether she arranged it or merely permitted it, she will not say. Her position is defensible either way — Lewis never asked.

When Lewis identifies the contact, they are not an enemy. Possibly useful.

---

## The Path Forward: The Ritual

The druid will meet Lewis on neutral ground first — Con will make contact when she is ready. Then:

1. **Journey into the forest** — the ritual requires Lewis to go to the druids' ground, not the other way around. Undertake a Journey, probably Dangerous given the forest and the vengeance faction watching.

2. **The sacred spring** — Lewis must be purified before entering the tomb. The spring will not let him lie. This is where his skepticism is tested hardest — a man going through the motions won't come out purified. Likely an Endure Stress moment: the cost is psychological. Lewis has things he hasn't admitted to himself about the Harrying.

3. **The tomb** — a thin place the druids have not dared re-enter. Lewis goes alone. Run as a series of Face Danger and Gather Information rolls without the full Delve framework. What he retrieves — or whether the act of retrieving it *is* the offering — to be discovered in play.

The sword is the obvious sacrifice. Uncomfortable given he just fished it from a lake.
