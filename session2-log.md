# Session 2 Log — Hammarby
*An Ironsworn solo campaign. Lewis of Mervik, lord of Hammarby.*

---

## The Standing Stone

Lewis told Theodor where he was going before he left. Brief, factual: north along Langsjön, the standing stone, when to expect him back. Theodor's expression did the thing it does. He didn't comment.

The walk north was easy at first — open ground between the tree line and the water. The lake flat and grey, no wind. The mist already thickening at the far end, earlier than it should. By the time Lewis found the stone the trees pressed almost to the bank and the light was nearly gone.

The stone was unmistakable. Taller than a man, grey, slightly leaning. Old in a way that made the estate's ruins look recent.

He waited. No torch.

He was staring out over the water, thinking about nothing in particular, when the voice came from directly behind him.

*"You found my charm, then."*

---

## Conlaoch

A man at the treeline. Lean, weathered, dressed for the forest. He hadn't drawn anything. He was just standing there, reading Lewis the way you read terrain.

Lewis reached for his sword out of reflex, caught himself, held out both hands instead.

*"My name is Lewis. Who are you?"*

A pause — long enough to be deliberate.

*"Conlaoch."*

Lewis didn't get it right. The name landed somewhere shorter, blunter. Conlaoch let it go without comment.

**Gather Information — Wits (2)**
*Action die: 2. Challenge dice: 4 and 2. Result: 4 vs 4 and 2 — Weak Hit. +1 Momentum (now 7).*

Conlaoch confirmed he had placed the charm — a warning, not a weapon. He explained the field: a wound torn open by the Harrying, a place where the boundary between the living world and the underworld had worn through. Aldric had walked into the middle of it. Whatever looked at him there had not meant to kill him. It had simply looked.

*"You want to know how to stop it. So do we."*

The 'we' was careful. Not quite an admission of anything.

*"But closing that wound requires something you cannot give. Something your king refused to give."*

## The Fifth Season

Lewis bristled at *your king.* A loyal man hearing a conquered man's grievance dressed up as cosmic fact.

*"Awfully convenient, this story of yours. A man is dead and somehow it is the king's fault? The king who took your land, leaving you with nothing but a thirst for revenge, is that it?"*

Conlaoch was quiet for a moment.

*"Yes. He took my land. My wife. My children."*

Flat. No performance. Just facts stated the way you state facts when you've had a long time to stop crying about them.

*"I placed that charm because I did not want another man to die the way they died. That is not revenge."*

He explained what the ritual required: the sacred marriage, the sacrifice, the king's participation — his willingness, not his death. That was all it had ever required. The king had refused. Called it barbarism. The fifth season did not care what he called it.

Lewis wanted to draw his sword. He let the urge pass through him instead.

**Face Danger — Heart (3)**
*Action die: 1. Challenge dice: 2 and 2. Result: 4 vs 2 and 2 — Strong Hit. +1 Momentum (now 8).*

*"Alright,"* he said finally. *"Say I believe you. What does the ritual require?"*

Something in Conlaoch shifted — barely visible. He hadn't been certain Lewis had it in him.

---

## The Druid

The ritual could be performed in full only with the king's participation, which was not forthcoming. But a thin place could be sealed separately — a smaller rite, a druid who knew the old ways, something given willingly to what lay beneath. Not a life. Something of value. Something with meaning.

There was such a druid. She had sealed thin places before, when the natives still had access to the land to do so.

*"Will she?"*

*"If you give her reason to."*

Lewis pushed back on the terms. Con had said the druids had been trying to reach someone who would listen — and here Lewis was. He was not about to walk into the forest on Con's schedule.

**Compel — Heart (3)**
*Action die: 6. Challenge dice: 9 and 3. Result: 9 vs 9 and 3 — Weak Hit.*

Con agreed to bring the druid to neutral ground. But he had a condition: the food the village had been leaving at the treeline — sustaining Con through the winter — continued. Openly. Lewis knowing about it and allowing it.

Lewis offered him a plot of land in the village instead. Con looked at him the way you look at someone who has revealed how little they understand.

*"I am not ready to live among your people. I may never be."*

Lewis accepted the terms.

*"I will find you at your estate when she is ready."* Con stepped back toward the trees. Paused. *"Lewis."* Said the name once, trying it out. Then the forest took him.

**Reach a Milestone — Inciting Vow**
*Progress marked: 1/10.*

## The Path Back

Lewis was halfway back along the lake when a figure stepped out of the treeline ahead. Poorly equipped — a woodsman's axe, clothes that had once been something better. Thin. Desperate.

*"Your purse. And your sword. Leave them on the ground and walk on."*

His voice was steadier than his hands.

**Enter the Fray — Heart (3)**
*Action die: 1. Challenge dice: 10 and 6. Result: 4 vs 10 and 6 — Miss. Foe has initiative.*

Lewis reached for his sword. The man read it, stepped sideways into the dark. Now Lewis had steel out and no idea where the man was.

The man spoke from the darkness, closer than expected.

*"You're the new lord. From the estate."*

He had been watching. For weeks, probably.

**Face Danger — Iron (2)**
*Action die: 6. Challenge dice: 7 and 9. Result: 8 vs 7 and 9 — Weak Hit. -1 Momentum (now 9).*

Lewis got his arm clear of the first axe swing but stumbled at the path edge. Off balance, the lake at his back.

**Clash — Iron (2)**
*Action die: 5. Challenge dice: 9 and 5. Result: 7 vs 9 and 5 — Weak Hit. 3 progress marked (3/10). Foe retains initiative.*

Lewis found the man's shoulder with his blade. The man hissed and staggered — but caught Lewis overextended. The counter caught Lewis's sword hand. The blade dropped into the mud, slid into the water.

*Pay the Price (rolled 30): Something of value is lost or destroyed. The sword is in Langsjön.*

Lewis was unarmed. The man had a dagger and was still standing.

**Secure an Advantage — Wits (2)**
*Action die: 2. Challenge dice: 6 and 2. Result: 4 vs 6 and 2 — Weak Hit. +1 Momentum (now 10).*

No one else in the trees. The sword was somewhere in the shallows — possibly retrievable.

**Face Danger — Iron (2)**
*Action die: 6. Challenge dice: 2 and 4. Result: 8 vs 2 and 4 — Strong Hit. +1 Momentum (capped at 10). Lewis has initiative.*

Lewis got inside the dagger's reach, one hand on the wounded shoulder, and wrenched the axe free. The man went to his knees.

**Clash — Iron (2)**
*Action die: 3. Challenge dice: 9 and 3. Result: 5 vs 9 and 3 — Weak Hit. 3 progress marked (6/10). Foe retains initiative.*

Lewis brought the axe haft down — not the blade, the butt end — caught the man across the temple. He went down. But Lewis was off balance again.

*Pay the Price (rolled 80): A surprising development complicates your quest.*

The man got back up with a dagger and a name for Lewis he hadn't earned yet.

*"You will never sleep easy, conqueror. We are everywhere."*

**Clash — Iron (2)**
*Action die: 4. Challenge dice: 2 and 2. Result: 6 vs 2 and 2 — Strong Hit with a match. 3 progress marked (9/10 — capped at 10). +1 Momentum. Lewis has initiative.*

Lewis caught the dagger on the axe haft, turned it clean, stepped inside, and put the man down hard. The match: something decisive. *We are everywhere* — not a boast. A fact. This was not a lone opportunist.

**End the Fight**
*Challenge dice: 6 and 7 vs progress 10 — Strong Hit.*

The fight was over.

---

## After

Lewis bound the man with his own belt, fished his sword from the shallows — cold water to the elbow, fingers finding the hilt caught against a submerged root, another inch and it would have been gone — and carried the unconscious man back to the estate over one shoulder.

Theodor was waiting in the doorway with a lamp and a carefully neutral expression.

Lewis told him everything. Con, the field, the fifth season, the ritual, the druid, the network of displaced natives watching the estate. Theodor listened without interrupting. Some things he did not write down.

His counsel: the druid comes, the ritual is performed, the field is sealed. Quietly. No record. If anyone asks, a problem with the soil, remedied. He could write the report in a way that would bore anyone who reads it into not reading it.

The larger question — the king, the ritual, what Con was really asking — that was not a problem for tonight.

*"One thing at a time, my lord."*
