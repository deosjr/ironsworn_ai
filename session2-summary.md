# Session 2 Summary — Hammarby

## Where We Left Off

Lewis went alone to the standing stone at the north end of Langsjön. He met Conlaoch — a native survivor of the Harrying, who had placed the warning charm at the north field and has been living in the forest near the estate, sustained by food left at the treeline by someone in the village.

Conlaoch confirmed what Edith had said about the field, and went further: the fifth season is a consequence of the king's refusal to participate in the sacred kingship ritual. The boundary between worlds is thinning everywhere, not just at Hammarby. The druids are performing what rituals they can but it is not enough without the king.

For the immediate problem — the north field — there is a path forward. A druid who knows a rite to seal thin places. Conlaoch agreed to bring her to neutral ground. His condition: the food the village has been leaving at the treeline for him continues, openly, with Lewis's knowledge and tolerance.

On the walk back, Lewis was ambushed by a displaced native turned bandit. The man knew Lewis by name and by sight — he had been watching the estate. His parting words before being knocked unconscious: *we are everywhere.* This suggests an organised network of displaced natives monitoring the estate, beyond what Con indicated.

Lewis retrieved his sword from the shallows of Langsjön, carried the unconscious man back to the estate, and told Theodor everything. Theodor's counsel: handle the druid quietly, keep no record, and deal with the larger question of the king another time.

The prisoner is bound outside the hall. A decision about what to do with him waits for morning.
