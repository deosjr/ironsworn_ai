# Session 4 Summary — Hammarby

## Where We Left Off

The north field is sealed. Bram is dead. Saoirse has returned to the forest. Lewis has sworn a new vow.

---

## What Happened This Session

Lewis retrieved the conduit from the tomb — an ancient rusted sword, significant not as a weapon but as a conduit between worlds. Moving it drew something through the thin place, which inhabited one of the tomb's dead. Lewis fought the Bonewalker at the tomb entrance, resealed the passage, and carried the conduit back to the clearing.

At the clearing, Conlaoch offered a partial admission: he had sealed the tomb without warning Lewis what was inside, not knowing the sealing would trap something in as well as out. Lewis accepted it and moved on.

Saoirse walked to the estate and sealed the north field using the conduit and the Tether ritual. The field is no longer a thin place. The conduit, its purpose spent, was thrown into Langsjön.

Saoirse confirmed that the thing that killed Aldric is still loose — sealed out of the field but not gone. It will find another thin place. The only permanent solution is the king's participation in the sacred kingship ritual, which Lewis is not yet ready or positioned to pursue. Instead he swore a Formidable vow to find and seal thin places before more people die, with Saoirse's help. She will make contact when she knows where the next one is.

Bram was executed that evening, off the books.

Maret witnessed the ritual and the conversation that followed. She now knows more than the other soldiers.
