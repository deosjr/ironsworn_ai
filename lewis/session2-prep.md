# Session 2 Preparation — Hammarby

## Where We Left Off
Lewis ended session 1 with a location and a deadline: follow Langsjön north along the eastern bank to a tall grey standing stone where the forest meets the water. Wait at dusk. Alone or with one trusted man. No torch.

Momentum: 6. Health: 5. Spirit: 5. Supply: 5.

---

## Opening Moves

Lewis tells Theodor where he is going before he leaves. He cannot afford Theodor not knowing.

Lewis goes alone, or with one unnamed soldier — leave this open, decide in play.

---

## Conlaoch (Con)

A native survivor of the Harrying. Lost his wife, children, and farm. Has not returned to the village but has not strayed far. Survives on wild game and forage. Knows the druids and how to contact them.

He placed the warning charm at the north field's edge. He has been watching events at the estate from the treeline — he knows about Aldric's death. He checks the standing stone each dusk as part of his routine; this is the first time a foreigner has shown up instead of a native contact with supplies.

**His attitude:** cold. 'I told you so.' He placed warnings that were ignored. He feels no guilt over Aldric and will not pretend otherwise. He will not volunteer help to a Conqueror lord.

**His name:** Conlaoch. Lewis will mishear it immediately — probably lands on *Con*. Whether Conlaoch corrects him says something about how much he still cares about being known.

He approaches carefully from the treeline. He already knows why Lewis has come. His opening move is likely: *you found my charm then.* Not a question.

---

## The Combat Trigger

After initial dialogue — Con admitting the charm, Lewis learning what Con knows — things go south. The trigger is a die roll: does Lewis lose his temper and accuse Con of murder? Heart roll, loyalty to Aldric pushing against better judgment.

Alternatively Con moves first, hostility toward Conquerors boiling over.

Either way: combat.

---

## Combat Outcomes

**Lewis kills Con**
Con has something on his body — a carved token, a marked object, something in the native tradition. Not a note. Lewis takes it back to Edith and admits what happened. That conversation will be harder than the first one. Edith becomes the path forward.

**Lewis wounds Con, Con escapes**
Con vanishes into the forest, hostile and warned. Lewis either tracks him on Con's ground at a disadvantage, or returns to Edith and admits he ruined it. Harder path forward either way.

**Con wounds Lewis, Lewis escapes**
Lewis retreats to the estate damaged. Has to explain himself to Theodor. Con could subsequently make contact on his own terms — he chose not to kill Lewis when he could have, which is itself a message.

**Con kills Lewis**
Death move. Handle per Ironsworn rules.

---

## The Through-Line
Edith is the consistent path forward regardless of outcome. Lewis will have to return to her. That second conversation will be shaped by what he did at the standing stone.
