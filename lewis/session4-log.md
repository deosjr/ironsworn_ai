# Session 4 Log — Hammarby
*An Ironsworn solo campaign. Lewis of Mervik, lord of Hammarby.*

---

## The Tomb

Lewis stood in the chamber with the rusted sword in his hand and felt the air change. Not cold exactly. Something without a name pressing in from everywhere at once, the way deep water presses. The thin place was responding.

**Face Danger — Heart (3)**
*Action die: 1. Challenge dice: 4 and 7. Result: 4 vs 1 and 7 — Weak Hit. Endure Stress (1) — Spirit: 3 → 2.*

He didn't run. That was all it was — a decision made somewhere below conscious thought, while something vast and without shape moved through the chamber around him. It wasn't interested in him. It was interested in the sword. He stood with it in his hands and let the presence pass through the space he occupied, and it was the most alone he had ever felt in his life.

Then it was gone. The air settled.

Lewis turned and walked back up the passage without looking behind him. Outside, he set the conduit carefully against the tomb wall and started replacing the stones.

He was halfway through when he heard a sound from inside. Movement. Slow, wrong-jointed. Getting closer.

**Secure an Advantage — Iron (2)**
*Action die: 1. Challenge dice: 9 and 6. Result: 3 vs 9 and 6 — Weak Hit. +1 Momentum (capped at 10).*

The stones burst outward — steadily, the way something strong and patient pushes when it has decided to move. Lewis stepped back into his stance. A man, once. Old bones in older clothes, moving with a deliberateness that had nothing human in it. The presence inside it hadn't learned to use the body yet — joints firing in the wrong order, head tilted at an angle that would have broken a living neck.

**Enter the Fray — Heart (3)**
*Action die: 4. Challenge dice: 4 and 10. Result: 7 vs 4 and 10 — Weak Hit. Take initiative.*

Lewis stepped into the thing's reach before it had fully found its footing, got his shoulder into it, drove it back against the tomb entrance. It didn't react to pain. But physics still applied.

**Strike — Iron (2) — Momentum burned (10 → 2)**
*Action die: 5. Challenge dice: 8 and 1. Result: 10 vs 8 and 1 — Strong Hit.*
*Swordmaster: +2 harm on burned momentum. Veteran: +1 momentum after reset, +1 on next move. Base harm 2 + 2 = 4 harm. Momentum: 2 → 3. Progress: 8/10.*

Lewis's sword went through it with less resistance than it should have — old bone, no muscle to speak of — and the thing staggered. Not from pain. From damage. A leg gave way.

**End the Fight**
*Challenge dice: 10 and 5 vs progress 8 — Weak Hit. Endure Harm (1). Health: 5 → 4.*

**Endure Harm — Health (5)**
*Action die: 1. Challenge dice: 8 and 9. Result: 6 vs 8 and 9 — Miss. -1 Momentum (now 2).*

Lewis put the thing down — sword through what had been a ribcage. The presence left the way it had come. But it had gotten something in first. A raking grab, fingers like wood, catching him across the side before he stepped clear. Not deep. But wrong.

He sat with his back against the tomb for a while. The side where it had caught him ached in a way that would be worse tomorrow. He turned the conduit over in his hands — whatever it was, it wasn't going to help him fight anything. Just old iron that something had used as a door.

The collapsed bones were visible just inside the entrance. He didn't look directly at them while he worked. He piled the stones higher than before, filling the opening completely this time, until there was nothing to see but a sealed mound. Then he picked up the conduit and his own sword and started back toward the clearing.

## The Return

Saoirse was where he'd left her. Conlaoch too. She looked at the conduit and something in her face settled — recognition, not relief.

Lewis set it down in front of her. Then he looked at Conlaoch.

*"You sealed that tomb."*

Conlaoch looked at the conduit, then at Lewis. *"Yes. You were not supposed to go in alone. That was not the arrangement."*

Saoirse explained without being asked: she had meant to take Lewis to the entrance and ward him going in. Conlaoch had sealed it when she told him she could not enter herself — he had not wanted something walking out unsupervised. She could see from Lewis's face that something had walked out anyway.

Lewis told them what happened. Saoirse confirmed it: the dead in the tomb didn't usually trouble anyone, but moving the conduit had opened a door and the nearest vessel was already there. She asked if he had resealed the tomb. He told her the stones were back in place.

Conlaoch said, without looking at him: *"I should have told you what the tomb contained. I did not know the sealing would hold something in as well as out."*

As close to an apology as he was likely to get.

Lewis looked at the druid. *"I held up my end of the bargain. What happens next?"*

*"We go to your field,"* she said.

## The North Field

Lewis returned to the estate ahead of Saoirse. He told Theodor what had happened. He told Rolf to give Bram his last supper. He took Maret back to the field.

Saoirse set the conduit upright in the earth at the field's centre and went to work. Maret stopped dead beside Lewis at the field's edge. He told her to leave the druid to it.

**Tether Ritual — Heart (4) + 1 (ritual bonus)**
*Action die: 1. Challenge dice: 1 and 2. Result: 5 vs 1 and 2 — Strong Hit. +1 Momentum (Saoirse's). The tether holds clean.*

It didn't look like much from the field's edge. Saoirse stood at the centre and went very still, and the mist at the lake's edge pulled back slightly — just slightly — like something exhaling. Then it was done.

She walked back to Lewis and held out the conduit. *"It is sealed. What you do with this is your concern now."*

**Fulfill Your Vow — Troublesome (Make the field safe) — Progress 9/10**
*Challenge dice: 7 and 7 vs 9 — Strong Hit with a match. 1 experience marked.*

**Reach a Milestone — Dangerous Vow (Deal with the thing that killed Aldric)**
*Progress: 2 marks added.*

## What Comes Next

Lewis asked the druid whether the thing that killed Aldric was gone too.

*"The wound is closed. What came through before the closing — that is another matter. It will find another thin place. There will be more thin places. Until the boundary is restored properly, this will not stop."*

She told him what restoration required: the king's participation in the sacred marriage ritual. His willingness, not his death. Just acknowledgment that the land is not his to take without cost. The druids had been trying to reach someone on the Conqueror side who would carry that message.

Lewis told her honestly that he was not that person. Not yet. He wasn't sure he believed her, even after everything he had seen. But if the thing was still out there, he still owed it to Aldric to make sure it didn't take another life. He asked her to help him hunt it down.

Saoirse was quiet for a moment. Then: *"The thing cannot be hunted. Not the way you mean. But thin places can be found before they fail. Sealed before something walks through. It is not nothing."*

*"I will help you find them."*

**Swear an Iron Vow — Formidable (Find and seal the thin places) — Heart (3)**
*Action die: 3. Challenge dice: 3 and 6. Result: 6 vs 3 and 6 — Weak Hit. +1 Momentum. Determined but the path forward is unclear.*

Lewis touched the iron at his belt and spoke the vow quietly. Saoirse watched without ceremony. When he was done she nodded once, picked up her wrapped cloth, and walked back toward the treeline without looking back.

---

## Maret

Maret had been silent through all of it. When Saoirse was gone she asked if the field was safe now. Lewis told her if the druid said so, that was a good sign — and asked if she had felt it, the moment the field was sealed.

*"Yes."* Then: *"Aldric felt it too. Before it killed him."*

Lewis told her they needed to make sure it didn't happen again. Not while they lived and breathed. Maret looked at him for a long moment. Then she nodded — small, definite.

She glanced at the conduit sitting at the field's edge. Lewis picked it up, felt its weight once, and lobbed it into Langsjön. It hit the water without ceremony and was gone.

They walked back to the hall. The field behind them, untilled but no longer wrong.

Bram was dealt with that evening. Off the books, as most necessary things were.
