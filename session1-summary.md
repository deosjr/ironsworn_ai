# Session 1 Summary — Hammarby

## Where We Left Off

Lewis has spent three weeks establishing himself at Hammarby. The hall is still a ruin but progress is being made. Then Aldric — one of his soldiers, known personally — is found dead at the edge of the untilled north field. No wound. No struggle. Just stopped.

Lewis examined the body, found a charm of tied rushes and a crow feather placed at the field's edge — a warning, not a weapon. He buried Aldric himself alongside his two remaining soldiers and Theodor, the older clerc.

He then spoke to Edith, the eldest woman in the village and its quiet authority figure. She told him:
- The charm was placed to warn Aldric away, not harm him
- The field has been wrong for two seasons — since before the Harrying, worsened by it
- Aldric's heart stopped because something from beneath looked at him directly
- The boundary between the living world and the underworld is thinning — she called it *the fifth season*
- The people who understand what walks that field are in the forest, not the village

Edith's directions: follow Langsjön north along the eastern bank to a grey standing stone where the forest meets the water. Wait there at dusk. Alone, or with one trusted man. No torch.

Lewis agreed. He has until dusk.
