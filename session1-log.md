# Session 1 Log — Hammarby
*An Ironsworn solo campaign. Lewis of Mervik, lord of Hammarby.*

---

## Arrival

Three weeks ago, Lewis rode north along a rutted track toward his new estate. Behind him: two clercs on a cart, three soldiers on foot. Ahead: a burned hall, a hostile village, and a border with nothing good beyond it.

He arrived to find the hall a stone foundation with a canvas stretched over part of it. A handful of peasants watched from a distance. Theodor, the older clerc sent ahead to prepare, waited at the village edge with papers and a carefully neutral expression.

Lewis dismounted Bally — awkwardly, as always — and addressed him.

*"Well met. I am Lewis, and uh.. this is my estate now. It seems it will need a lot of work. But let us get out of the weather before we talk of such matters."*

Theodor's neutral expression flickered. Just once.

They went inside. Theodor presented his inventory: thirty-one able-bodied peasants, eleven fields of reasonable quality — three untilled, peasants refusing to explain why — supplies adequate for the season, the foundation serviceable if timber could be sourced before winter.

Lewis spent three weeks learning the shape of the place. Which fields drained badly. Which peasants would meet his eye. How far the forest pressed in from the north. Progress, of a kind.

---

## The Death of Aldric

On the twenty-second morning, Theodor appeared in the doorway while Lewis was eating.

*"My lord. It's Aldric. He went out yesterday to walk the northern boundary — said he wanted to look at the untilled fields before he reported to you."*

A pause.

*"We found him this morning. At the field's edge. No wound. No sign of struggle."*

Lewis stood and slammed the table.

*"Out with it, man! What do you mean? Aldric's dead? How?"*

Theodor took a small step back. *"I don't know how, my lord. He was on his back, facing the sky. No mark on him. The ground around him undisturbed."* He hesitated. *"The peasants won't go near him. They won't touch the body. They don't seem surprised."*

Lewis collected himself. Straightened his shirt.

*"Thank you, Theodor. I will see to him at once. Was it you who found him?"*

*"No, my lord. Two of your soldiers found him at first light. They came to me before waking you. They are shaken."*

---

## The Field

Lewis walked out alone into the morning fog. Around the hill, east toward where the field met the lake. Langsjön was grey and still, disappearing into mist two hundred yards out. He couldn't see the far end.

Aldric was face up in the furrow between the field and the lakeside grass. Arms at his sides. Like he had lain down.

Lewis had seen plenty of dead men. This one was wrong in a way that was hard to name. No violence to him. No last struggle written in the dirt. His face was calm.

The grass directly beneath him was dry. Not frost-dry. Just dry. In a field that had been wet all spring.

Lewis took his time. Walked the field edge, crouched, looked. He found one other thing: a single set of footprints leading from the village track to where Aldric lay. One set in. None out. Aldric's own boots matched the prints. He had walked here alone and not left.

At the field's edge, where the grass met the first furrow, Lewis found a small bundle — tied rushes, a crow feather, something dark and dried he couldn't identify. Placed deliberately. Recently.

He didn't know what it meant. The uneasy feeling was worse now, crouching there with the lake at his back.

He pocketed the bundle.

---

## The Vow

Lewis stood over Aldric for a long moment. The lake behind him. The untilled field in front. The estate that would fail if that field stayed empty through harvest.

Quietly, to no one:

*"I will find what did this. And I will put a stop to it."*

**Swear an Iron Vow — Formidable — Heart (3)**
*Action die: 6. Challenge dice: 8 and 3. Result: 9 vs 8 and 3 — Strong Hit. +2 Momentum (now 5).*

The vow settled. Lewis straightened, and the fog seemed no worse than it was.

---

## The Burial

Lewis buried Aldric himself, alongside his three soldiers and Theodor — who did not complain about the digging despite his age and his clerc's hands. They found a spot at the field's edge, high enough that spring water wouldn't reach it.

Nobody said much. Lewis said a few words over the grave when it was done. Nothing formal. Just Aldric's name, where he came from, that he was a good soldier and didn't deserve this end. The kind of words a foot soldier says over another foot soldier.

The soldiers looked steadier afterward. Theodor caught Lewis's eye with a slight tilt of his head toward the village.

---

## Theodor's Counsel

Before approaching the village, Lewis asked Theodor what he knew.

*"I am not the right man to interpret their customs, my lord. But there is a woman. Edith. Oldest in the village — she was here before the Harrying. The others defer to her without making a show of it. If anyone will speak plainly it is her, but only if she decides to."*

He glanced at the bundle in Lewis's hand.

*"I would not show that to just anyone in the village."*

---

## Edith

Lewis found her at home, sitting by a quietly smouldering fire. She was small, sharp-eyed, hands busy with mending. She didn't stand when he filled her doorway.

She waited.

*"Edith? My men say you know what happens in this village better than anyone. Pray tell, what does it mean when I find this next to one of my men? I found him dead in a field this morning, with no way of knowing what happened to him but this."*

He took out the charm.

Edith looked at it for a long moment. Her hands stopped moving. Then she looked up at him — not at his face exactly, but at him.

*"Sit down."*

Lewis felt frustration cross his face. But he knew better than to argue with wise women. He grabbed the stool by the door and moved it closer to the fire, folding himself onto it. It was a bit too small for him. There was no one to comment on the comedy of it.

Edith took the charm and turned it over in her fingers.

*"This was not put there to harm your man. It was put there to warn him away."* She set it on her knee. *"Someone in this village knew he was going to that field and tried to stop him. Without speaking to him directly. They were afraid to speak to him directly."*

She looked at Lewis steadily.

*"The field is wrong. Has been wrong since before your people came. The Harrying made it worse — things were disturbed that should not have been disturbed. We do not go there. We have not gone there for two seasons now."*

*"Wrong how?"* Lewis managed.

*"Wrong like the fifth season."* She read his face and tried again. *"You have noticed the mist. How it lingers past when it should lift. How some mornings feel like the world forgot to wake up properly."*

She looked at the fire.

*"The boundary between this world and what lies beneath it — it is not as solid as it was. Things leak through. Not things you can fight."* She glanced at his sword. *"Not things you can bury and be done with."*

She held the charm back out to him.

*"Your man walked into a place where the boundary is thin. Whatever he met there — it was not one of us."*

Lewis absorbed this.

*"So how did he die then?"*

**Gather Information — Wits (2)**

Edith was quiet for a moment.

*"His heart stopped. That is what happens when something from beneath looks at you directly. Not a curse. Not a blade. Just — looked at."*

Then she closed up slightly. Not hostile, but done.

*"I have told you what I know of the field. What walks there, why it walks there — that is not my knowledge to give."*

Lewis leaned forward.

*"A man died here. You know what this looks like, or what rumours will have spread come end of day. I suggest you tell me whatever you know or there will be trouble, you know that as well as I do."*

**Compel — Heart (3)**
*Action die: 6. Challenge dice: 1 and 8. Result: 9 vs 1 and 8 — Weak Hit.*

Edith looked at him for a long moment. Not frightened. Weighing him.

*"I know,"* she said quietly.

She looked at the fire.

*"The ones who know what walks that field — they are not in this village. They are in the forest. I can tell you where to look. But if you go to them with a sword on your hip and soldiers at your back, you will find nothing. They will vanish and you will come home no wiser than you left."*

She met his eyes.

*"Go alone. Or take one man you trust. No more."*

*"How do I know you are not leading me directly to my death?"*

Edith almost smiled.

*"You don't. Same as I don't know you won't burn this village down before winter."*

She let that sit.

*"But you came here alone. You sat on that ridiculous stool. You asked instead of demanded — mostly. I am telling you what I know because a dead field and a dead soldier helps none of us. Not you, not mine."*

She picked up her mending again.

*"We both need that field producing come harvest. That is enough common ground for now."*

Lewis nodded.

*"That will have to do. Tell me where to find them and I will find out if you are telling me the truth."*

Edith nodded once.

*"Follow Langsjön north along the eastern bank. Where the forest meets the water there is a grey stone, taller than a man, standing alone. Wait there at dusk. Don't bring a torch."*

She returned to her mending. He had been dismissed.

---

## End of Session

Lewis stepped back out into the grey morning with a location, a condition, and a decision to make before dusk. Somewhere north along the lake, at a standing stone, someone was waiting — or would be, when the light failed.

He had until then.
